\begin{center}
	\Large
	\textsc{Grid-free 2D Poisson equation solver}
	
	\large
	Version: \today
\end{center}

These notes are based on 
\emph{Grid free method for solving the Poisson equation} by J. Kuhnert and S. Tiwari (2001), Berichte des Fraunhofer ITWM, Nr. 25.


We want to solve
\begin{equation}
\nabla^2 f = s
\end{equation}
in a 2D domain $(y,z)$
with possible boundary conditions $f=a$ and $\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}y=b$ or $\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}z=b$.
Dirichlet boundaries are passed as additional points at which the solution is not found but instead given.
For each other point, an array of booleans determines whether and which Neumann BC they have.
Periodic boundaries can currently be prescribed along $y$ for a fixed distance.

For each point $p$, we find the smallest possible number of neighbour points $i\in \{1,k \}$ and consider the Taylor series approximation
\begin{equation}
f_i = f_p 
+ \Delta y \frac{\mathrm{d}f}{\mathrm{d}y}
+ \Delta z \frac{\mathrm{d}f}{\mathrm{d}z}
+ \frac{\Delta y^2}{2} \frac{\mathrm{d}^2f}{\mathrm{d}y^2}
+ \frac{\Delta z^2}{2} \frac{\mathrm{d}^2f}{\mathrm{d}z^2}
+ \Delta y \Delta z \frac{\mathrm{d}^2f}{\mathrm{d}y \mathrm{d}z},
\end{equation}
where $\Delta y = y_i - y_p$ and $\Delta z = z_i - z_p$. 
Further,
\begin{equation}
\frac{\mathrm{d}^2f}{\mathrm{d}y^2} +  \frac{\mathrm{d}^2f}{\mathrm{d}z^2} = s_p
\end{equation}
and potentially
\begin{equation}
\frac{\mathrm{d}f}{\mathrm{d}y} = b_y \quad \mathrm{and/or} \quad \frac{\mathrm{d}f}{\mathrm{d}z}=b_z.
\end{equation}


We combine the system into
\begin{equation}
\begin{bmatrix}
s_p \\ 
b      \\ 
f_i      \\ 
\vdots
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0        & 0        & 1            & 1            & 0 \\
0 &  \{0, 1\}        & \{0, 1\}        & 0            & 0            & 0 \\
1 & \Delta y_i & \Delta z_i & \Delta y_i^2/2 & \Delta z_i^2/2 & \Delta y_i \Delta z_i \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \\
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
f_p \\ 
\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}y      \\ 
\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}z      \\ 
\mathrm{d}^2f/\mathrm{d}y^2  \\ 
\mathrm{d}^2f/\mathrm{d}z^2  \\
\mathrm{d}^2f/(\mathrm{d}y\mathrm{d}z)
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
or $K=A O$.
The $(k+2) \times (k+2)$ weight matrix is
\begin{equation}
W=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \hdots \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \hdots \\
0 & 0 & w_i & 0 & 0 & \hdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & \ddots & 0 & \hdots
\end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
with weights $w_i=\varepsilon/(\Delta y^2 + \Delta z^2 + \varepsilon)$, where $\varepsilon=\alpha \Delta y^2 \Delta z^2$ measures a typical distance to neighbours using the closest point of all $k$ and a suitable $\alpha$ (for example $\alpha=1$).

We update the field values $f$ using the first entry in $O$ after solving
\begin{equation}
O=\left[ \left( A^T W A\right)^{-1} A^T W \right] K 
\end{equation}
at each point $p$.
